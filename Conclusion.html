Climate Change Effect on Evolutionary Rates

Evolutionary Rates

  • Evolutionary rated is dependent on 2 things:
  • The likelihood of a change to the genome
  • And the population's ability to overcome koinophilia
  • Koinophilia is the instinct of an animal to not breed with an animal that has observable changes to their DNA or mutations
  • In extreme circumstances, both of these can change so that the animal can quickly change to evolve for its environment
  • The likelihood of genetic change is raised meaning more animals are changing
  • And koinophilia decreases towards those with favorable traits
  • A changing climate is one of those extreme circumstances and so we would probably see more animals changing and changing faster so the evolutionary rates will increase
